# Project Assignment: Design a Document Management System for a Company

## Objective  
The goal of this assignment is for you to design a comprehensive document management system (DMS) for a hypothetical (or real) company in a specific business field. You will describe the company and its business processes, analyse its document/workflow needs, and propose a structured DMS solution (e.g. using SharePoint or a similar platform). ## Requirements  

1. **Company description & business context**  
   - Choose (or invent) a company in a specific field (e.g. manufacturing, healthcare, legal, logistics, marketing, etc.).  
   - Provide background: what the company does, size (approximate number of employees), main departments / divisions, and a high-level overview of its business processes.  
   - Identify what kinds of documents the company handles (e.g. contracts, invoices, HR records, reports, technical drawings, correspondence, etc.).  

2. **Current (or hypothetical) document/workflow analysis**  
   - Describe how documents are currently managed (or *would be* managed without a formal DMS): where documents are stored, who has access, how versioning works (or doesnâ€™t), how sharing and collaboration happen, what the pain points are (e.g. lost documents, outdated versions, redundant copies, security issues, difficulty finding documents).  
   - Highlight the main problems and inefficiencies that motivate the introduction of a DMS.  

3. **DMS requirements & goals**  
   - Specify the objectives for the new DMS (e.g. centralised storage, version control, controlled access, better search, workflow automation, audit trail, improved collaboration, regulatory compliance, backup/archiving, etc.).
   - Define any non-functional requirements: security/access control, user roles and permissions, metadata/tags, retention/archiving policy, backup strategy, usability, scalability, etc. 

4. **Proposed DMS solution design**  
   - Choose a DMS platform (e.g. SharePoint, or another), and justify why it is suitable for this company.  
   - Provide a proposed folder structure and metadata taxonomy (or tagging scheme) for storing documents.  
   - Define user roles and permissions (who can read, edit, approve, archive, delete, etc.).  
   - Describe workflows for common business processes (e.g. document creation, review & approval, versioning, sharing, archiving).  

5. **Example usage scenario**  
   - Provide at least one concrete example scenario (e.g. a contract being created, reviewed, approved, archived â€” or an invoice workflow, or HR onboarding documents flow).  
   - Show step-by-step how documents flow through the system (who does what, which folder they go into, what metadata is applied, how versioning and permissions work).  

6. **Migration / Implementation considerations** *(optional but recommended)*  
   - If the company currently has documents in paper or in unstructured digital files, describe how you would migrate them to the DMS (e.g. scanning, digitizing, categorizing).
   - Suggest training or adoption plan for employees, and how to ensure consistent usage of the system (governance, guidelines, audits) over time. 

7. **Deliverables**  
   - A report that includes: company description, requirements, proposed DMS design, folder/metadata structure, user roles/workflows, and example usage scenarios.  
   - A diagram or simple visualization (flowchart, tree structure, table) of the folder hierarchy and/or workflow(s).  
   - A small prototype skeleton (folders + sample documents or placeholder files) showing the structure in the chosen DMS platform (or as a folder hierarchy).  

8. **Evaluation criteria**  
   - Clarity and completeness of company description and business context.  
   - Soundness of requirements analysis â€” do the identified pain points and needs justify the DMS design.  
   - Quality and logic of proposed DMS structure: folder / metadata design, roles & permissions, workflow definitions.  
   - Realism and completeness of usage scenario(s).  
   - Consideration of implementation, migration, governance or user adoption challenges (if included).  
   - Quality and readability of documentation (Markdown, diagrams, structure).
